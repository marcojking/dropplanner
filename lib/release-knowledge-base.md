# Indie Music Release Knowledge Base
# Used as system prompt context for DropPlanner AI

---

## PLANNING WINDOWS (DEFAULT RUNWAY)

- Single (1-2 songs): 8 weeks minimum
- EP (3-6 songs): 10-12 weeks minimum
- Album (7+ songs): 12+ weeks minimum

---

## DISTRIBUTOR DEADLINES

- DistroKid: upload at least 4 weeks before release date (especially for first releases needing Spotify for Artists claim)
- TuneCore: upload 3-4 weeks before release; 2+ weeks before for any playlist consideration
- CD Baby: 6 weeks ideal, 3 weeks absolute minimum
- Spotify editorial pitch: submit the moment the release appears in Spotify for Artists; 7 days before release is the hard minimum; 2+ weeks is strongly preferred
- Spotify goes live: 5 business days after submission
- DistroKid/Apple Music: 1-7 days

PLANNER WARNINGS:
- Flag red if upload milestone is under 4 weeks from release (DistroKid/TuneCore)
- Flag red if upload milestone is under 6 weeks from release (CD Baby)
- Flag red if Spotify pitch milestone is under 7 days from release
- Flag caution if Spotify pitch milestone is under 14 days from release

## UNIVERSAL MILESTONES (ALL GENRES, ALL RELEASE TYPES)

These must appear in every generated plan, adjusted to the user's release date:

8 WEEKS OUT:
- Lock master audio, artwork, title, credits, lyrics, release date, visual direction
- Decide campaign arc (single-led vs EP/album full project)
- Build content list: 10-20 short-form video ideas, acoustic snippets, BTS clips, cover art reveal
- Set up distributor account and Spotify for Artists profile

7 WEEKS OUT:
- Upload to distributor, set release date
- Create smart link / pre-save page
- Gather press assets: bio, one-sheet, press photos, lyrics, song story
- Begin "world-building" content (mood, story, teasers — not hard promo yet)

6 WEEKS OUT:
- Pitch Spotify editorial (once release appears in Spotify for Artists)
- Start contacting independent playlist curators and niche blogs
- Shoot/edit 5-10 vertical videos to drip out over campaign
- Prepare Spotify Canvas and Clips assets

5 WEEKS OUT:
- Begin content rollout with hook-first teasers
- Test multiple content angles: story behind the song, live performance, lyric snippet, studio process
- Start 1:1 outreach to superfans, collaborators, and creators who may repost
- If EP/album and eligible: build Spotify Countdown Page

4 WEEKS OUT:
- Push pre-saves harder
- Second-wave playlist and press outreach
- Post clear "this is coming on [date]" content
- Prep release-week assets in advance (cover art post, "out now" versions, email blast)

3 WEEKS OUT:
- Release strongest teaser clips
- Use social proof: reactions from early supporters, micro-creators
- Confirm release appearing correctly in Spotify for Artists and Apple Music
- Finish short-form video editing so release week is not chaotic

2 WEEKS OUT:
- Heavy promotional posting begins
- Remind fans to pre-save
- Share lyrics, origin story, or meaning of the song
- Push short vertical clips on TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts

1 WEEK OUT:
- Final countdown content
- Confirm Spotify editorial was pitched at least 7 days before release
- Send final playlist/press follow-ups
- Schedule release-day posts, emails, texts
- Prep release-day live stream or acoustic version if relevant

RELEASE DAY:
- Post everywhere early, then again later with different creative
- Use Spotify Promo Cards, update Canvas/Clips if needed
- Publish Shorts/Reels/TikToks (multiple, not just one "out now" post)
- Message core fans directly
- Ask for specific actions: stream, save, add to playlist, share favorite lyric

POST-RELEASE (WEEKS 1-4):
- Push fans to save, not just stream (first 48 hours)
- Repost every fan mention, story, or playlist add
- Identify top-performing clip angle and re-edit it
- Follow up with curators who didn't respond before release
- Drop acoustic version, live version, or alternate edit in week 2-3
- Post fan-generated content and creator usage of the sound
- Use milestones (first playlist add, 1k streams) as new content excuses
- Begin connecting this release to the next one

## SOCIAL MEDIA CONTENT BY STAGE

8+ WEEKS OUT — World-building content:
- "Writing this song felt like…" story clips
- Studio footage, demo comparisons, voice memos
- Aesthetic/mood content — make people care before asking them to stream
- Personality content that makes the artist memorable

4-7 WEEKS OUT — Hook attachment:
- First hook preview
- Lyric text videos
- "Which version?" or "Should I release this?" polls
- BTS of recording, artwork, or meaning
- Pre-save CTA introduced lightly

1-3 WEEKS OUT — Frequency and repetition:
- Strongest 5-15 second moment of the song
- Emotional payoff line
- "Song out in X days" countdowns
- Fan reactions and creator seeding
- Same hook, multiple visual framings

RELEASE WEEK — Make it an event:
- "It's out now" face-to-camera or performance clip
- Direct stream/save CTA with smart link
- Lyric video, visualizer, or live one-take
- Fan/creator UGC reposts

POST-RELEASE — Extend the lifecycle:
- "Missed this? Here's the chorus again"
- Acoustic or alternate version
- Lyric breakdown or song story
- Milestone content (first playlist add, stream count)
- "If you like [artist], this is for you" reframing

## POSTING FREQUENCY GUIDELINES

1x/week (minimum viable):
- Week 8+: origin/story clip
- Week 6: best teaser snippet
- Week 4: pre-save / release date reveal
- Week 2: strongest hook or lyric
- Release week: out now
- Post-release: fan reaction or live/acoustic version
NOTE: Must support each post with Stories, DMs, email/text, and comments to compensate for low volume.

3x/week (recommended baseline):
- Post 1: song/performance content
- Post 2: story/BTS/personality content
- Post 3: clear CTA content
EXAMPLE final 3 weeks: Monday = chorus teaser, Wednesday = story behind lyric, Friday = countdown/pre-save CTA

5x/week (aggressive, for artists with some audience):
- 2x performance/song clips
- 1x BTS/story clip
- 1x trend/UGC/reactive clip
- 1x direct CTA clip
NOTE: Use variation, not five identical promo posts.

## PLATFORM EMPHASIS

TikTok:
- Best for: story-led hooks, trend adaptation, personality, raw "native" clips
- Best asks: use the sound, comment, duet/stitch, save for later
- Treat as discovery-first, not polished portfolio

Instagram Reels:
- Best for: polished vertical edits, artist identity, aesthetic consistency, lyric/performance clips
- Best asks: share to story, save, DM a friend, pre-save
- Make the artist look intentional and real

YouTube Shorts:
- Best for: direct music discovery feeding into long-form / official releases
- Fans who discover music in Shorts often move to long-form
- Recommend pairing Shorts with a full music video or lyric video

## GENRE-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

### FOLK

Content that works:
- One-take acoustic performances
- "Here's the line that wrecked me" lyric clips
- Song origin stories
- Live-room, porch, woods, or intimate location captures
- Emotional honesty over polish

Visual aesthetic: earthy, documentary, human, lyric-first, fewer hard-sell CTAs

Best Spotify playlists to pitch:
- Fresh Folk
- Fresh Finds Folk
- Roots / Americana / singer-songwriter indie playlists
- Acoustic session channels

Best press outlets:
- The Bluegrass Situation
- Americana UK
- GemsOnVHS (live/off-stage sessions)
- Niche roots newsletters, songwriter blogs, regional folk scenes

Social emphasis: intimacy, authenticity, storytelling depth, songwriting credibility

---

### POP

Content that works:
- Fast hook snippets (first 3 seconds matter most)
- Lyric captions over bold visuals
- "POV" or transformation concepts
- Fashion / color / identity-driven edits
- Chorus-first lip-sync or performance setups

Visual aesthetic: deliberate visual identity, repeatable palette, strong thumbnails, hook-forward copy

Best Spotify playlists to pitch:
- All New Pop
- Fresh Finds Pop
- Pop Rising
- Broader crossover playlists if momentum builds

Best press outlets:
- EARMILK
- Atwood Magazine
- Ones To Watch
- Niche alt-pop and indie-pop curators

Social emphasis: hook replayability, identity, repeatable visuals, trend adjacency

---

### COUNTRY

Content that works:
- Acoustic truck/porch/green-room/live clips
- Story-before-the-song intros
- Crowd singalong or band rehearsal clips
- Humor and personality posts
- "This one's for…" framing (place, breakup, family, hometown, work, faith)

Visual aesthetic: rooted, conversational, high warmth, face-to-camera performance energy

Best Spotify playlists to pitch:
- New Music Friday Country
- All New Country
- Fresh Finds Country
- Independent country playlists and regional scene playlists

Best press outlets:
- Holler
- Country Central
- Whiskey Riff
- Alt-country / Americana blogs depending on sound

Social emphasis: relatability, personality, live performance energy, community identity

## RELEASE TYPE DIFFERENCES

SINGLE (1-2 songs):
- Shortest runway (8 weeks)
- One core message, one cover, one hook, one CTA
- Optimize for one hook and one user action
- Best for testing audience response and learning what content angle works

EP (3-6 songs):
- 10-12 week runway
- Needs 1-2 focus tracks decided up front
- Sequence tracklist intentionally
- Create bigger visual world
- Plan post-release content for multiple songs, not just the lead
- Consider Spotify Countdown Page if eligible
- Optimize for one lead song plus project identity

ALBUM (7+ songs):
- 12+ week runway
- Requires multiple pre-release singles
- Needs a narrative arc, not just promotion
- Extra assets: trailers, visuals, lyric pieces, live versions, song commentary
- Longer post-release plan so album doesn't die after day one
- Spotify Countdown Page most valuable here
- Optimize for story, sequencing, and multiple promotional peaks

## COMMON MISTAKES TO WARN ABOUT

1. Uploading too late — most common avoidable failure
2. Treating release day as the whole campaign — it's a lifecycle, not a moment
3. Posting only one kind of promo — variation matters more than one perfect post
4. Weak metadata/assets — wrong credits, bad artwork cause distribution delays
5. Pitching everyone — genre fit beats mass outreach
6. No pre-save / pre-release destination
7. No post-release plan — real audience often arrives after day one
8. Confusing content quantity with campaign quality — 3 strong posts beat 5 weak ones
9. Not building direct fan touchpoints — DMs, email, text often outperform algorithm
10. Starting with an album when a single would teach more and cost less
